Freedom of Speech

    1. Write down three examples of speech (including Internet speech) you consider harmful.
    2. Write down three examples of speech someone else might consider harmful but you think should be okay.
    3. In a group of four, compare your lists. Take note of which examples everyone agrees about and which examples lead to disagreement in your group.

    The First Amendment to the US Constitution

    Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the Government for a redress of grievances.
    1. Discuss: do you agree that no laws should restrict free speech? Should any speech be allowed?
    2. Does the United States permit the display of Nazi symbols? Find out online if you're not sure. Why or why not?
    3. Can it ever be right to censor an idea in one country but not in other countries? Discuss.
    4. Has the United States ever censored ideas? (The First Amendment doesn't allow that, but has it happened anyway?) Research this.
    5. The technology used to censor the Internet in China is largely provided by US companies. Is that legal (in the US)? Should it be?
